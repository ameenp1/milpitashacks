**Eligibility & Process** CalWORKs is designed to help parents or caretakers of kids under 18 and people who are pregnant. Eligibility will depend on things like your household income, resources, and age. 

## **Eligibility** 

Your eligibility is based on a few general factors. A local expert with your county will work with you to see if you’re eligible. 

**Age** 

Both adult caretakers or parents and children may be eligible for CalWORKs. 

If you’re an adult, you must have or take care of, or be pregnant with, children 18 and under (or under 21 if the child is disabled). 

If a child is under 18, to get benefits they must either be pregnant or have a parent who is unemployed, disabled, absent, or deceased. 

If a child is below age 6, they must have all standard immunizations, and if above age 6, they must be attending school.

## **Application Process** 

Applying should take 30–60 minutes once you’ve collected your information. Your local rep can help you through the process. 

## **Gather documentation** 

First, we’ll need to verify some information: 

**Identification** 

A photo ID or an official document that verifies your identity: 

## **Examples:** 

- Birth certificate 

- U.S. passport 

- Driver’s license 

- Government ID card 

- Permanent Resident card (Green Card) 

## **Proof you live in California** 

Something that confirms your address. 

## **Examples:** 

- Rental agreement 

- A bill 

- Driver's license 

- Identification card 

## **Money you earn** 

Any form of payment or income you receive. 

## **Examples:** 

- Recent pay stubs 

- Copy of child support check 

- Letter of award of benefits (for Social 

- Security, unemployment, disability, etc.) 

- Letter from employer 

## **Housing and utility costs** 

Any expenses related to housing. 

## **Examples:** 

- Rental agreement or receipts 

- Utility bill 

- Home or renter's insurance bill 

- Hotel or motel receipt 

## **Resources** 

Proof of property or assets that could be converted to cash. 

## **Examples:** 

- Vehicle registration 

- Bank, loan, or insurance statements 

- Sponsor statement form 

- Property deed 

## **Other expenses** 

Any expenses that cover basic needs. 

## **Examples:** 

- Child care receipts 

- School expenses 

- Child support payments 

- Medical expenses 

- Proof of pregnancy from doctor or clinic 

- Statement by your doctor or disability funding by an agency (for Social Security, unemployment, disability, etc.) 

## **Immunization records for children under 6** 

Proof of required shots from a doctor or clinic. 

## **Examples:** 

- If your child does not have a shot record, the county can help you get proof. If you don’t want to get your child immunized, you must provide a statement that shots are against your beliefs, a statement explaining why your child can't get shots, or a statement from the doctor that shots are not available. 

## **Relationships (if applicable)** 

Confirmation of parental relationships of guardians. 

## **Examples:** 

- Court papers (divorce, guardianship, etc.) 

- Birth certificate 

- Marriage certificate 

- Domestic partner certificate 

## **Crime survivor status (if applicable)** 

Supporting documentation that proves you were a victim of a crime. 

## **Examples:** 

- Letter from the Office on Trafficking in Persons 

- A sworn statement from you or someone who knows you’re a victim of human trafficking 

- Letter from a professional you get help from, like social services, a support group, or your legal or medical aid 

- Evidence of the crime such as police, government agency, or court records or files, or physical evidence or news articles
